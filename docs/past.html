LANDFIRE’s Biophysical Settings (BpSs)

BpS models represent the vegetation system that may have been dominant on the landscape prior to Euro-American settlement. These models are based on a combination of the current environment and an approximation of the historical disturbance regime. Read more.

Check out this 3-minute tutorial for a quick refresher on the basics of Biophysical Settings Models.

Use this page as a starting point to explore the most prevalent BpSs in the SRFSN Northern New Mexico Plateau Subregion. In addition to the charts and maps, the historical disturbance data can be directly accessed using this site.

Summary

  • Prior to European colonization the top 10 BpSs covered almost all of the subregion, with the Inter-Mountain Basins Greasewood Flat being the most dominant at ~22%.
  • The Inter-Mountain Basins Semi-Desert Grassland had the most fire, with ~ 12,000 acres annually prior to European colonization.
  • While fire was an important disturbance historically, the Rocky Mountain Montane Riparian Systems experienced the most disturbance with ~ 127,000 acres of ‘wind/weather/stress’ (i.e., flooding) occuring every year.
